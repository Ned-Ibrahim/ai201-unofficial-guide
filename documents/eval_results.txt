Q1: What does USCIS require for post-completion OPT eligibility?

GROUND TRUTH: Maintained F-1 status for one full academic year, employment directly related to major, DSO recommendation entered in SEVIS, file Form I-765 within the window; 12-month OPT cap.

SYSTEM ANSWER:
According to [2], to be eligible for post-completion OPT, a student must: 
1. Have maintained F-1 status for a minimum of one academic year, 
2. Have confirmed eligibility for graduation with their academic department/unit, 
3. Not have accrued 12 months or more of full-time Curricular Practical Training at their current degree level, 
4. Not have previously completed OPT at the same degree level or a higher degree level, 
5. Submit the OPT application to USCIS as early as 90 days prior to their Program End Date and no later than 60 days after their Program End Date, and 
6. Be in the U.S. to file Form I-765. Additionally, [1] and [5] provide general information about OPT eligibility, but [2] provides the most detailed list of requirements.

The form required for STEM OPT training is Form I-983, Training Plan for STEM OPT Students [1], [2], [3], [4]. This form is completed by both the student and their employer, as they must work together to finalize the plan [2].

Q3: What are the unemployment day limits on standard OPT versus STEM OPT?

GROUND TRUTH: 90 days on standard post-completion OPT; 150 days total with the STEM OPT extension.

SYSTEM ANSWER:
According to [1] and [2], the unemployment day limits are as follows: 
For standard OPT, you may be unemployed for up to 90 days [1], whereas for STEM OPT, you may be unemployed for an additional 60 days, for a total of 150 days [1] during the combined periods of OPT and STEM OPT [2].

Q4: Can I be self-employed on OPT, and does the answer differ for STEM OPT?

GROUND TRUTH: Self-employment is allowed on standard OPT (degree-related, 20+ hrs/wk, proper licenses); on STEM OPT it is barred — a bona fide employer-employee relationship, E-Verify employer, and signed I-983 are required.

SYSTEM ANSWER:
Yes, you can be self-employed on OPT, as long as your work meets certain criteria, such as being related to your field of study and meeting the minimum 20-hour-per-week requirement [1, 2, 5]. However, self-employment is not permitted during the 24-month STEM OPT extension [1, 2, 5]. The STEM OPT extension has stricter requirements, including supervised employment and E-Verify registration, which make self-employment highly restrictive [2].

Q5: Do I need an EIN to file taxes as a freelancer on OPT?

GROUND TRUTH: No. An EIN is optional for a sole proprietor (SSN/ITIN works); obtaining one does not violate F-1 status. (NOTE: this is tax-law info not in the corpus — expected to be a coverage-gap failure / refusal.)

SYSTEM ANSWER:
According to [1], to ensure compliance, you should formally register your business, but it does not explicitly state that you need an EIN to file taxes as a freelancer on OPT. However, [2] mentions that an employer must "Have a valid Employer Identification Number (EIN) issued by the IRS for tax purposes" for STEM OPT, but this is for employers, not freelancers. I don't have enough information on that.
